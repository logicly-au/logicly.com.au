The idea of 'doing work impactfully' has been informally embedded in Logicly since 1996 when Adam and Simon set up their operations on a mission to improve the way data was being collected, used and interpreted.

They were determined, and still are, to build a company to be proud of.


## What are B Corps?
<a href="https://www.bcorporation.net/en-us/certification">Certified B Corporations are companies that meet verified standards of social and environmental performance, transparency and accountability. </a> 

The certification is administered by B Lab through a rigorous assessment and verification process. It took a lot of work from our small team to document and collate the evidence required to demonstrate that we meet these standards. 

Certified B Corporations are also part of a global movement of businesses working together to help build a more inclusive, equitable and regenerative economy.


## So why become a B Corp?
<div class="blog-text-list">
<ol>
<li><strong>We can always do better.</strong><p>
<p>The B Impact Assessment helps organisations measure, manage and improve their impact across governance, workers, community, customers and the environment. It enables us to benchmark ourselves against similar organisations, identify our biggest gaps, and gives us a best practice framework for ongoing improvement.</p> 
<p>Certified B Corps are required to undergo the verification process every three years in order to recertify. Working towards and maintaining our certification helps us continue improving how we operate and the impact we have.</p></li>
<li><strong>We agree that business can be a force for good.</strong></p>
<p>We’re a privately owned business that believes we can look after our customers and team, do our best for the planet, and support our communities.</p> 
<p>We’re proud of the projects we work on and the clients we work with. Logicly’s genesis was the recognition that data can and should be collected, used and interpreted to drive better outcomes for people. Its very first project was supporting the new PTSD research facility at the Austin Repatriation Hospital, and three of our current major projects are dedicated to using data to improve mental health services across Australia. We also support research projects evaluating suicide prevention initiatives and help understand how public transport is used. We love working on projects with real public value.
</p></li>
</ol>
</div>

## What it means for our customers

Becoming a Certified B Corporation reflects our commitment to considering how our decisions affect our customers, employees, communities and the environment. It helps keep us accountable as we build a responsible, purpose-driven business and continue improving how we operate.


## Curious?

If you're curious about businesses working together to build a more inclusive, equitable and regenerative economy, we encourage you to explore the <a href="https://bcorporation.net/">B Corp website</a>. And if you're curious about our experience becoming a Certified B Corporation,  <NuxtLink to="/contactus">get in touch</NuxtLink> and we’d be happy to share more about the journey.
